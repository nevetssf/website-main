## Layoff

Silicon Valley can be a brutal place. When you're part of a team, you belong. When you're laid off, you suddenly don't. And when the whole kit-and-kaboodle is gone, there's nothing left but the buildings — steel and structures that don't move on the way people do.

## Village Flowers

Village flowers are one of the pleasures of living in San Francisco. A neighbor kept potted flowers on the sidewalk — until they didn't. The flowers are gone now, but the photographs remain. What's left in the neighborhood is more subtle; there isn't the same drama in humble flowers.

[![Village Flowers](http://nevsf.smugmug.com/Flowers/Village-Flowers/50004039-003/96022090_hyumC-M-2.jpg)](https://nevsf.smugmug.com/Flowers/Village-Flowers)

## Orchids

Orchids are strange things. They look like they should belong to some other creature entirely. Every year I go to the Northern California Orchid Exhibition near Fort Mason to see what the growers have done — and to admire the sheer devotion of the growers. Orchids have blood and veins; we just have to find them.

[![Orchids](http://nevsf.smugmug.com/Flowers/Orchids/d1004703-001/95790385_5PNMa-M.jpg)](https://nevsf.smugmug.com/Flowers/Orchids)

## Abandoned and Unloved

An ongoing project. My partner once said that things were brought to life through use and abuse — that a worn, damaged object had been animated by the world in a way a pristine one hadn't. I've been photographing stuffed animals ever since. This one was found at a construction site in Amsterdam.
