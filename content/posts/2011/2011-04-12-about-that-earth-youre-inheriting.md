The meek shall inherit the earth — though that seems increasingly unlikely. By the time that inheritance comes due, the wealth of the world will have stripped it of its natural resources, dissolved the ozone layer, and poisoned its soils. The younger generations will also inherit the tax burden.

The rich will get richer and the poor will get poorer. The timeframe for democratic accountability in the United States is too long. Whether elected officials truly represent their constituents is an open question.

America might benefit from the kind of institutional reform that would let citizens remove ineffective leaders. But that's a different revolution from Corporate America, whose entrenched interests would complicate any such change.
